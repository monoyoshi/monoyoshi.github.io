Welcome to the Hyperfixation Corner

Regardless of whatever you might've seen in my profile, I mostly do everything as a hobby. I make things that tickle this silly autistic brain of mine. I cannot stop thinking about my interests, so I love making things that relate to them.

As a web developer, I specialize in vanilla HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. I focus on these because of how easily accessible they are, and because I can't afford a server all by myself.

As a graphic-design-leaning artist (hey, it really is my passion!), I enjoy making clean pictures that work very well when displayed on its own, whether it be as a small component for a bigger project or as a physical item like a shirt design.


VQUBER
"Remember Marth from the hit game Dragalia Lost? This is him now."
Main page

Originally known as Project DROCSI, this has been my passion project ever since Dragalia Lost announced the event Fire Emblem: Lost Heroes in April 2019. It stood for DRagalia Lost Original Character Self Insert (yeah yeah, it's goofy, but it stuck at the time). The character themselves prefer to go by the name REN.

Initially inspired by Marth Fire Emblem (and later on, Farren Dragalia Lost), REN is a character that represents most if not all of me. They're a monstrous fusion of both dragon and human who just wants to live on their own terms.

hotaru(n)
"hahahahaha"
Main page GitHub repository

A Discord bot that does a lot, from unserious things like laughing at people, to serious things like calculating the 36th number in the Fibonacci sequence (it's 14930352). hotaru(n) is also the predecessor to some of the other projects I have here. In a sense, he's like my sandbox...if sandboxes could be sassy and crave world domination. He is also my first-born, so a lot of curses follow him. He doesn't mind, though.

Made using Javascript (Node.js) on a boring after-school November afternoon in 2016, hotaru(n) uses Discord.js to interact with Discord's API. And no, you can't add him onto your server. I mean, unless you clone his code, but I am not liable for any damages he might cause.

Oh, and the pages here would double as a cooler, flashier, and more concise version of the help command, but I'm not really in my hotaru(n) messing era (for that command) right now.
